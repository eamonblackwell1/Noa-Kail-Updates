01
Garden Yokohama Best Value

Yamete Jubankan

A 1967 Western-style mansion in Yokohama's historic Yamate district, steps from the Foreign Cemetery and the old Western settlement. This is a small-wedding specialist with exclusive one-couple-per-day use and a maximum capacity of 33 guests. The English garden features a fountain, rose beds, and a red-carpet aisle where couples hold outdoor ceremonies between the flowers.

This is the single venue in our research where budget, garden aesthetic, small-wedding expertise, and guest count all align without compromise. French cuisine is prepared in-house, and past couple reviews consistently praise the food quality. The neighbourhood itself has real character: tree-lined streets, Western-era mansions, and views over Yokohama harbour.

02
Glass Chapel Daikanyama Premium

Ristorante ASO

Daikanyama's most celebrated Italian restaurant occupies a Showa-era Western mansion surrounded by 300-year-old zelkova trees. The centrepiece is a glass-walled garden chapel with an 8-metre ceiling and a retractable roof that opens to the sky. It's the most visually dramatic ceremony space in this price range.

Part of the Hiramatsu fine-dining group, ASO's Italian cuisine is world-class (their karasumi pasta is legendary). The Family Wedding Plan is remarkably comprehensive: ceremony, full Italian course, drinks, cake, attire with a 40% dress discount, hair and makeup, florals, snap photography with all-cuts DVD, gifts, and venue fee are all included.

03
Forest Kamakura Unique

Koga-tei

A 110-year-old Western mansion sitting on 1,500 tsubo of forested grounds, just a 5-minute walk from Kamakura Station. The ceremony takes place in a forest clearing under a massive camphor tree, with birdsong and dappled light as the only backdrop. It's the most "only in Japan" outdoor setting of any venue in this research.

The venue operates as a modern Spanish restaurant day-to-day (cuisine is excellent) and offers two-group-per-day exclusive use for weddings. The interior is genuine: original hardwood floors, a working stone fireplace, large windows overlooking the garden. The atmosphere lands somewhere between a European country estate and a Kamakura temple garden.

One honest caveat: the small wedding plan has officially ended, and pricing for standard weddings trends higher than the other options on this list. Reviews note costs can escalate, particularly on attire. Juri would need to negotiate a custom package to bring this within budget. That said, for the right couple who values the setting above all else, it's worth the conversation.

04
Garden Daikanyama Budget-Friendly

Restaurant Madame Toki

A standalone 1978 French restaurant in Daikanyama that looks like it was transplanted from the Parisian countryside: orange stucco facade, green shuttered windows, mahogany interiors, marble floors. The garden hosts jinzen-shiki ceremonies with live accordion and viola. Maximum 30 seated guests on the main dining floor, with exclusive use.

The critical budget advantage: attire and decorations can be brought in free of charge, and the venue pricing is transparent and straightforward. Room fee plus per-person food and drinks, 10% service charge, done. No hidden mandatory add-ons. This gives us maximum control over costs and the most comfortable budget margin of any venue on this list.

Budget note

Three of the four venues can deliver a 20-guest wedding under $20,000, with Madame Toki offering the most headroom for honeymoon savings. The biggest cost levers are guest count (each additional guest adds roughly $150–170 in food and drink), attire choices (using venue-included or base-level attire vs. upgrading), and weekday vs. weekend timing. At 30 guests, Madame Toki and Yamete Jubankan remain within budget. ASO's Family Plan caps at 20.
